The Impact of Climate Change on Marine Ecosystems

The world's oceans are undergoing rapid transformation as a result of climate change. Warmer sea temperatures have triggered widespread coral bleaching events, putting immense pressure on the biodiversity that reef ecosystems support.

Beyond warming, the absorption of excess atmospheric carbon dioxide is making ocean water more acidic. This acidification weakens the protective shells of many marine organisms, including mollusks and several species of plankton that form the base of the marine food chain.

Marine biologists have also documented clear shifts in fish migration routes, with many species relocating toward cooler waters in response to rising temperatures. These shifts disrupt long-established food webs and create real challenges for the commercial fishing industry.
